Main projects
4MOST classification
4MOST is a Galactic survey in ongoing development that aims at observing millions of objects with spectroscopy. I am leading the classification unit within the consortium.
Gaia validation and Hybrid Catalogs
As part of the Gaia collaboration (DPAC-CU9), the hybrid catalog aim to provide specific catalogs of Gaia sources combined with other wavelengths and information available. Such work is extremely important also for the internal validation of the astrophysical parameters derived by the consortium (DPAC-CU8).
Automated detection of star cluster in surveys
Algorithm combining of photometric and velocity measurements with density profiles. (Fouesneau et al, in prep)
Star cluster disruption from the photometric end-point
Statistical modeling and measurement of cluster disruption effect on present day cluster populations. (Fouesneau et al, in prep)
Inferring Dust Distribution on 20pc scale from resolved stellar populations in M31
Statistical modeling and measurement of dust distribution from CMD fitting of resolved stars in M31 through PHAT survey. (Dalcanton et al, 2015)
Stellar object characterization from spectro-photometric measurements
Calibrating spectra with photometry in order to derive more reliable stellar parameters (Weisz et al, in prep)
Bayesian extinction and stellar parameters fitting code (BEAST) (Gordon et al, 2016)
Determination of stellar parameters and dust properties using individual stars (Gordon et al, in prep)
